The Secret

All Ceylon Spices

✦

We use only 100% authentic Ceylon spices, sourced directly from Sri Lanka's finest estates. These ancient aromatics are the soul of every dish.

Ceylon Cinnamon
Kurundu · කුරුඳු

True cinnamon, native only to Sri Lanka. Delicate, sweet, and subtly complex — considered the finest cinnamon in the world. Used in our curries, rice dishes, and desserts for a warm, aromatic depth.

Sweet Aromatic Warm Antioxidant
Ceylon Red Chilli
Miris · මිරිස්

Bold, vibrant heat with a fruity undertone. Sri Lankan chillies deliver a full-bodied spice that builds slowly and lingers beautifully. The heart of our signature fiery curries and kottu.

Fiery Bold Fruity Rich Heat
Ceylon Curry Leaves
Karapincha · කරපිඤ්ඤා

The soul of Sri Lankan cooking. Fresh curry leaves release a citrusy, floral aroma when fried in oil — they form the aromatic base of nearly every authentic dish we prepare.

Citrusy Floral Aromatic Fresh
Ceylon Turmeric
Kaha · කහ

Vivid golden root with an earthy, peppery warmth. Our Ceylon turmeric boasts higher curcumin content than imported varieties, giving our fried rice and curries their signature golden hue and health benefits.

Earthy Golden Anti-inflammatory Warming
Ceylon Black Pepper
Gammiris · ගම්මිරිස්

The "king of spices" — Sri Lanka's black pepper is renowned for its intense, complex heat with floral and citrus notes. Used liberally in our kottu, noodles, and marinades for a distinctive punch.

Intense Floral Citrus Notes Sharp
Green Cardamom
Enasal · එනසල්

Intensely aromatic with eucalyptus and sweet floral notes. Ceylon cardamom pods are hand-picked and sun-dried. A key player in our biryanis, masala blends, and desserts.

Floral Eucalyptus Sweet Digestive
Ceylon Cloves
Karabu · කරාබු

Intensely pungent and warming with a numbing quality. Sri Lankan cloves are prized for their high eugenol content, making them more potent than other varieties. Essential in our biryani and spice blends.

Pungent Warming Numbing Medicinal
Ceylon Cumin
Suduru · සූදුරු

Earthy, nutty, and slightly bitter with a warm undertone. Ceylon cumin seeds are slow-roasted before grinding to release their full aromatic potential. The backbone of our curry powder blends.

Earthy Nutty Roasted Digestive
Pandan Leaf (Rampe)
Rampe · රම්පේ

The "vanilla of Asia" — pandan leaves impart a sweet, grassy, and floral fragrance. Used fresh in our coconut rice and desserts, rampe is what makes Sri Lankan rice unmistakably fragrant and special.

Floral Sweet Grassy Fragrant
Goraka (Gamboge)
Goraka · ගොරකා

A uniquely Sri Lankan souring agent made from dried garcinia fruit. Goraka gives our fish curries and black curries their signature tangy depth that cannot be replicated with vinegar or tamarind.

Sour Tangy Unique Ceylon-only
Fenugreek Seeds
Uluhal · උළුහල්

Slightly bitter, nutty seeds that temper in hot oil to release a rich, maple-like aroma. Fenugreek is the secret behind the depth in our slow-cooked curries and is prized in traditional Ceylon medicine.

Bitter Nutty Maple-like Medicinal
Yellow Mustard Seeds
Abba · අබ

Tiny golden seeds that pop and splutter when added to hot oil, releasing a nutty, pungent flavour. The traditional tempering base for all our Sri Lankan curries, dhal, and coconut chutneys.

Nutty Pungent Tempering Pops in Oil
